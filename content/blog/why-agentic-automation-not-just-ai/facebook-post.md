## Post

A question I get asked regularly: "Can't you just get AI to build the tool for you?"

Sometimes you can. But for an automation your business is going to depend on every day, there's a difference between something that looks finished and something that keeps running reliably when nobody's watching.

This is why I build on a visual automation platform rather than generating custom code. Every step is visible — you can see exactly what your automation does, and when something goes wrong, you know exactly where and why.

The full explanation is in a new post on the website — it covers why "AI-built" doesn't always mean "reliable", and what actually makes automation trustworthy for a small business.
