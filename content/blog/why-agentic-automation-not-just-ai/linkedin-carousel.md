### Slide 2 — What vibe coding actually is

**Headline:** What "vibe coding" actually is.

**Body:**
You describe what you want in plain English.
AI writes the code.
A working app appears in seconds.

It's real. It's impressive. A quarter of Y Combinator startups now have codebases that are 95% AI-generated.

For developers who know what they're doing, it's a powerful accelerant.

**Design notes:** Neutral, fair introduction. Don't dismiss it outright here. Set up the nuance.

### Slide 3 — The 70% problem

**Headline:** The 70% problem.

**Body:**
AI gets you to 70% very fast.

The last 30% — making it reliable, secure, and maintainable when something goes wrong at 9pm on a Tuesday — still requires real engineering knowledge.

That's the part your business depends on.

**Design notes:** Visual could show a progress bar stopping at 70%. Shift to a slightly more cautionary tone.

---

### Slide 4 — What the research shows

**Headline:** What the research actually shows.

**Body:**
AI-co-authored code contains **1.7× more significant issues** than code written by experienced developers.

Between **40–60% of AI-generated code** contains security vulnerabilities.

The software can work. That's not the problem.
The problem is what it looks like under the bonnet.

**Design notes:** Big stat numbers. Bold the 1.7× and 40–60%. Research-backed, credible tone.

### Slide 5 — Why this matters for your business

**Headline:** This isn't a developer problem. It's your problem.

**Body:**
A tool that breaks quietly is worse than no tool at all.

If your email sorting fails silently, you think your inbox is under control when it isn't.

If your call handling stops working on a bank holiday, you don't know until a customer complains.

Custom code means custom maintenance. Forever.

**Design notes:** Relatable examples. Business owner perspective, not a developer's. Slightly urgent tone.

**Headline:** Why I use n8n instead.

### Slide 7 — The honest case for AI code tools

**Headline:** To be fair: AI code tools are impressive.

**Body:**
I use them. For one-off integrations, complex custom logic, or specific infrastructure work, they're often the right choice.

The difference is that I understand what I've built, I can maintain it, and I'm accountable for it continuing to work.

Vibe coding is a tool for people who know what they're doing with it.

**Design notes:** Balanced, honest. Shows credibility by acknowledging the nuance rather than dismissing AI entirely.
