# LinkedIn Post — Why I Build Automations Rather Than Just Use AI

**Source post:** [Why I build automations rather than just use AI to do everything](https://www.observeautomation.com/blog/why-agentic-automation-not-just-ai/)

## Post

There's a question I get asked fairly regularly: "Can't you just ask AI to build the tool?"

The honest answer is yes, sometimes you can. Tools like Claude Code and Cursor are genuinely impressive, and I use them. But for the automation a business is going to depend on every day, "built quickly by AI" and "built to keep running reliably" are two different things.

Research published in 2025 found that AI-generated code contains roughly 1.7 times more significant issues than code written by experienced developers. Between 40% and 60% of it contains security vulnerabilities. The headline finding from one widely-read piece put it bluntly: AI gets you to 70% very fast. The last 30% — the part that makes something genuinely reliable — still requires real engineering knowledge.

This matters for businesses, not just developers. A tool that breaks quietly is worse than no tool at all. If your email sorting automation silently fails, you think your inbox is under control when it isn't.

This is why I build on n8n rather than generating custom code. Every step in a workflow is a visible block. Customers can see exactly what their automation does, follow the logic without any technical background, and understand what happens when something goes wrong. When a step fails, n8n tells you which one and why — no guesswork.

The AI does the thinking. The platform handles the reliability. That distinction matters when your business is depending on it.
