## Post

How much of your morning disappears into email?

For most business owners I speak to, it's ten minutes on a quiet day — sorting, labelling, deciding what needs attention and what can wait. On a busy day, when the phone is ringing and customers are waiting, that sorting gets rushed. And somewhere in the noise, something important gets missed.

The problem isn't that people are disorganised. It's that a busy inbox is genuinely hard to manage when you're also trying to run a business.

I built an automation that reads incoming emails and sorts them automatically — applying labels in Gmail based on what each email actually says, not just who it's from. Supplier invoices go to one place, customer enquiries to another, advertising gets moved out of the way entirely.

The categories are managed in a spreadsheet, not in code. If something needs changing, it's a straightforward edit.

Nothing happens that can't be undone. The automation sorts and labels — it doesn't reply, delete, or act on anything without you. There's a draft-reply feature built in, but it stays switched off by default.

The running cost for a typical business inbox works out to a few pounds a month.

If you're spending time on this every day, it might be worth ten minutes to find out whether automation could handle it instead.
