## Post

Nearly half of all invoices sent by UK small businesses are paid late.

Not occasionally. On average.

The average small business is owed £21,400 in overdue payments at any given time. And the business owner is spending 86 hours a year chasing it — more than two full working weeks, every year, on emails and phone calls to people who already owe them money.

The reason it's hard to fix isn't effort. It's that chasing invoices competes with everything else, it feels uncomfortable, and it's easy to talk yourself out of the first reminder until the invoice is already three weeks overdue.

What actually works is a consistent process that kicks in automatically — a friendly nudge on day one, a follow-up at day seven, something firmer at day fourteen, and a final notice at thirty days. Professional, timely, and logged so you always know what's been sent.

That process doesn't require a credit controller or an accounts team. It's a straightforward automation that connects to your invoicing system, runs every morning, and handles the chasing without you having to think about it.

The businesses that get paid fastest aren't necessarily the ones with the most assertive owners. They're the ones with the most consistent process.
