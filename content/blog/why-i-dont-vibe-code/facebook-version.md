Here is something I get asked about: do I use AI to build the automations I deliver to clients?

Yes and no.

I use AI tools constantly, for research, for testing, for my own personal projects. But I do not "vibe code" client solutions, where you describe what you want, let an AI write it, and ship it without fully understanding what it produced.

The reason is simple. Small businesses need automations that keep working as the business changes. A new supplier. A staff member leaving. A tweak to how you handle invoices. If the automation is a black box that nobody understands, every small change becomes a call back to the developer. That is a cost and a dependency you do not want.

What I build is meant to be understood. Visual workflows, clear logic, documentation, and a walkthrough so you know what you have got. The goal is that, in time, you could make small changes yourself, or at least know enough to ask the right questions.
