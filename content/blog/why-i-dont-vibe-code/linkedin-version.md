Vibe coding is fast, impressive, and genuinely useful.

I use it all the time, for personal projects and testing ideas. But I do not use it for client automations. Here is why.

When you vibe code something, you get something that works. What you do not always get is something anyone can explain. The logic was generated rather than designed, and when something changes, because your business evolves, because an API updates, because a new team member joins, nobody knows where to start fixing it.

For a small business, that is a dependency, not an asset.

The automations I build are visual, documented, and designed to be understood. I use n8n, which means the workflow logic is readable without being a developer. I walk every client through what was built and why. And I design for the day when I am not involved any more.

Most clients never need to edit their own workflows. But they could. That distinction matters more than it sounds.
