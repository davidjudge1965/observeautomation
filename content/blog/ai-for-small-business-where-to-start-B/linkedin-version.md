# LinkedIn Post — AI for small business: where to actually start

---

41% of UK small businesses say they don't know where to start with AI.

That's not a technology problem. It's a communication problem.

Most of the advice available starts with the wrong question. Which tool should I use? What's the difference between automation platforms? Should I be looking at AI agents?

Wrong starting points, all of them.

The right question is much simpler: what's currently costing you time or money that you'd rather not be doing?

Start there. Not with the technology — with the problem.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

A florist missing roughly ten calls a day while serving customers. Each one a potential order, sometimes worth thousands. She didn't need a platform or a strategy. She needed something to answer the phone when she couldn't.

A sole trader spending two hours every Sunday evening sorting through a week's worth of emails. Supplier updates, customer enquiries, junk, invoices — all mixed together. He needed the urgent ones to surface without him having to wade through everything else.

A small business owner manually copying data from emailed invoices into a spreadsheet. Half an hour a day, every day, for years.

None of these are exotic problems. And all three were solved with tools that cost a fraction of what the wasted time was worth.

82% of UK businesses that have adopted AI say it boosted their productivity. 76% report improved profitability. These aren't businesses that overhauled everything at once. They started somewhere specific and built from there.

A practical place to begin: write down the three tasks you do most regularly that you find most tedious. Not the hardest, not the most important — just the most repetitive and least rewarding.

Chances are, at least one of them can be automated.
