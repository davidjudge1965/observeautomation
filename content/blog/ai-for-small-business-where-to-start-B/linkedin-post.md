## Post text (copy/paste ready)

41% of UK small businesses want to use AI.

Most of them don't know where to start.

That's not their fault. The advice isn't aimed at them.

It's either breathlessly enthusiastic ("AI will transform everything!") or deeply technical ("here's how to choose between AI models"). Neither helps if you run a plumbing business or a flower shop.

Here's what actually works. Swipe through. ↓

---

A florist was missing 10 calls a day while serving customers. Each call could have been a £50 order. Or a £2,000 wedding enquiry.

A sole trader was spending two hours every Sunday sorting through a week's worth of emails. Junk, invoices, supplier updates, genuine enquiries — all mixed together.

A business owner was manually copying invoice data into a spreadsheet. Half an hour, every day, for years.

None of these are exotic problems. And all three were solved with tools that cost less per month than a business lunch.

---

The right starting point isn't "which AI tool should I use?"

It's "what is currently costing me time or money that I would rather not be doing?"

Start there. The tool becomes much less important once you know the problem.
