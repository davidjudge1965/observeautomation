## Slides

---

### Slide 1 — Cover

**Headline:** Nearly half of UK small businesses want to use AI. Most don't know where to start.

**Subtext:** The advice isn't helping. Here's what actually works.

**Design notes:** Clean, text-forward slide. Bold headline on a light or mid-tone background. Professional feel. ObserveAutomation logo bottom right.

---

### Slide 2 — The problem with the advice

**Headline:** The advice is aimed at the wrong audience.

**Body:**
"AI will transform your business."
"Here's how to choose between AI models."
"Build agentic workflows."

None of this helps if you run a plumbing company in Buckinghamshire or a flower shop in Bedfordshire.

**Design notes:** Use a contrast between typical AI advice (complex/buzzy) and the reality for small business owners. Could show two columns.

---

### Slide 3 — The wrong question

**Headline:** Most people start with the wrong question.

**Body:**
"Which AI tool should I use?"
"What's the difference between automation platforms?"

The right question is much simpler:

**What is currently costing you time or money that you would rather not be doing?**

Start there.

**Design notes:** Two sections. First in muted/smaller text, second in bold/larger text. Strong contrast.

---

### Slide 4 — What the problems actually look like

**Headline:** Real problems. Real people.

**Body:**
A florist missing 10 calls a day while serving customers. Each could be a £50 order. Or a £2,000 wedding.

A sole trader spending two hours every Sunday sorting emails. Supplier updates, junk, invoices, all mixed together.

A business owner copying invoice data into a spreadsheet. Half an hour, every day, for years.

**Design notes:** Three short vignettes. Each in its own box or section. Real, relatable, specific.

---

### Slide 5 — Starting small is not thinking small

**Headline:** Starting small is not thinking small.

**Body:**
82% of UK businesses that adopted AI say it boosted their productivity.
76% report improved profitability.

They didn't overhaul everything at once.

They started with one problem. Solved it. Measured the difference. Moved on.

**Design notes:** Large stat figures. Bold the 82% and 76%. Reassuring, momentum-building tone.

---

### Slide 6 — The three barriers (and why they're smaller than you think)

**Headline:** The three things that hold people back:

**Body:**
1. "I don't know which use cases to go for." Start with your own frustrations. Done.
2. "I don't have the expertise." Modern tools need no technical knowledge.
3. "It'll cost too much." Many of the best tools cost less per month than a business lunch.

**Design notes:** Numbered list. Each barrier gets a quick dismissal. Conversational tone.

---

### Slide 7 — The practical starting point

**Headline:** A practical place to start:

**Body:**
Write down the three tasks you do most regularly that you find most tedious.

Not the hardest. Not the most important.
Just the most repetitive and least rewarding.

Chances are at least one of them can be automated.
The average UK business saves £29,000 a year and reclaims 122 hours of admin time per employee.

**Design notes:** Action-oriented. Clear instruction. Finish with the stats as a payoff.
