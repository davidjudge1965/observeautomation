## Post

Most small business owners know they miss calls. Very few have actually sat down and worked out what each one costs.

The maths isn't complicated. Take a trades business taking ten to fifteen calls a day, missing 40% of them because they're on the tools, with a client, or simply not near their phone. At a conservative average job value of £200 and a 30% conversion rate, that's around £6,000 a month going to whoever picked up instead.

For florists, the numbers look different but the problem is identical. A missed Saturday morning call during wedding season isn't a missed bouquet — it's a missed consultation that could be worth thousands.

The reason it's hard to fix isn't effort or organisation. It's that one person cannot simultaneously do the work and answer the phone. The phone doesn't stop ringing because you're busy.

There are three practical options: hire a receptionist (£20,000–£35,000 a year), use a call-answering service (cheaper but impersonal — a generic call centre with no knowledge of your business), or use an AI voice receptionist (from £200 a month, answers immediately, available 24/7, greets callers in your business's name and answers common questions).

The AI option isn't right for every business. If your work runs on complex relationship calls, you need a human. But for initial enquiries, appointment requests, and routine questions — which is the majority of inbound calls for most trades and service businesses — it handles it well.

The honest starting point is the calculation: how many calls do you miss in a typical week, and what's a conservative estimate of what each one is worth?
