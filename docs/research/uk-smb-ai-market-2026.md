# UK SMB AI & Automation Market — Research Briefing 2026

Researched: 2026-03-28. Sources: British Chambers of Commerce, Lloyds, FSB, techUK, ONS, Yell/Google UK, Henley Business School, Onrec.

---

## Adoption rates

- **35%** of UK SMEs are actively using AI (2025, BCC/Intuit) — up from 25% in 2024
- **45%** had integrated at least one AI tool by 2024, up from 25% in 2022
- Professional services lead (37–46% adoption); construction lags (1%)
- Only **33%** have no plans to adopt AI — down from 43% last year

## The "stuck in the middle" majority

- **41% of UK small businesses want to use AI but don't know where to start**
- 31% are scared to implement AI despite acknowledging the benefits
- 55% haven't yet been convinced of the benefits at all
- 61% feel overwhelmed by the pace of AI development
- 56% are excited about AI's potential

## Barriers to adoption (in order)

1. Can't identify use cases / don't know where to start (39%)
2. Lack of expertise (35%)
3. Cost (30%)
4. Uncertainty about ROI (25%)
5. Data security concerns (31%)

## Sentiment

- 49% believe AI would help take their business to the next level
- 49% cite increased efficiency as the top expected benefit
- 44% want to reduce time spent on low-value, repetitive work

## Business impact (when SMBs do adopt)

- **82%** of businesses using AI say it boosted productivity (Lloyds)
- **76%** reported improved profitability (Lloyds)
- Average savings: **£29,000/year** and **122 hours of admin time** per employee (Yell/Google UK)
- Innovative SMEs recorded **14.8% revenue growth** (FSB)
